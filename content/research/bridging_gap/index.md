<p style="text-align: justify;">
<em>Research, research everywhere, but application somewhere!</em> Hydrology has no shortage of scientific advances, but many never fully reach the people, agencies, and communities who can use them. My work focuses on bridging that gap by translating satellite remote sensing, hydrologic modeling, and geospatial science into practical tools, trainings, and decision-support systems for water management, agricultural resilience, and sustainable resource planning.
</p>

<span style="color: var(--white);"><strong>Projects</strong></span>

<ul>
  <li><span>Operationalized the Integrated Rice Advisory System (IRAS) on Department of Agricultural Extension (DAE), Bangladesh servers and trained teams for long-term government use.</span></li>

  <li><span>Trained Bangladesh Water Development Board (BWDB) engineers to use SWOT satellite data for water-resources monitoring.</span></li>

  <li><span>Trained Department of Hydrology and Meteorology (DHM) Nepal and BWDB Bangladesh teams to use optical and radar satellite data for surface-water monitoring.</span></li>

  <li><span>Trained Pakistan Council of Research in Water Resources (PCRWR) on using future SWOT observations for national water-resources projects.</span></li>

  <li><span>Supported satellite-based irrigation and crop-water monitoring workflows for farm-level decision-making in South Africa.</span></li>

  <li><span><strong>...and many more efforts translating satellite science into real-world water and agricultural decisions.</strong></span></li>
</ul>
